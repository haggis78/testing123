Overview: Eurasian Travelers

In this project, we traced the itineraries of more than a dozen medieval Eurasian travelers whose routes corresponded, more or less, to the "Silk Roads". Below you will find an annotated list, a timeline, and an interactive map.

List of travelers

  • Xuanzang, Buddhist monk and scholar. Traveled from China to India and back, 629-645 CE.
  • John of Plano Carpini, Franciscan Friar who traveled to the Mongol capital of Karakorum from 1245-1247. Carried letter from Pope Innocent IV to the Mongol Leader.
  • Francisco Balducci Pegolotti, Flortentine Merchant. Compiled Silk Road routes from various merchants into a mercantile guidebook, 1290-1347CE.
  • Marco Polo, Venetian explorerer. Began his journey in 1271 and returned to Constantinople in 1295 after traveling along the Silk Road and recording his journey.
  • William of Rubruck, Flemish friar under order of King Louis IX. William traveled from Constantinople to Karakorum and back to convert the Great Khan Mongke to Catholic Christianity, 1253-1255 C.E.
  • Rabban Bar Sawma, Chinese Christian monk who lived from 1220 to 1294. Traveled the silk road as a religious teacher who traveled from china to europe as a religious ambassador.
